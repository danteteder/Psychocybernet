# Daily Decision System: Durov-Style for Nordspike + Psychocybernet

## System Philosophy

> *"The goal is to do first and then feel, not feel and then do."* — Pavel Durov

This system is designed to:
1. **Eliminate fear/greed-driven decisions** (the two enemies of freedom)
2. **Train the discipline muscle** daily (300 push-ups + decision reps)
3. **Reinforce principles over profit** in every business choice
4. **Create compounding intelligence** through decision journaling
5. **Integrate with existing tools** (Psychocybernet, Telegram, Hermes)

**Outcome:** After 90 days, you'll make faster, clearer, more principled decisions—automatically.

## Component 1: Morning Clarity Block

### Physical Discipline (Non-Negotiable)

**Why:** Trains the "discipline muscle" that transfers to business decisions.

| Exercise | Reps | Purpose |
|----------|------|---------|
| Push-ups | 300 | Daily consistency, upper body strength |
| Squats | 300 | Daily consistency, lower body strength |
| Cold shower | 60 sec | Stress resilience,意志力 (willpower) |
| Gym | 5-6x/week, 1-2 hrs | Compound movements, progressive overload |

**Implementation:**
- **Timer app:** Simple countdown (no phone = use physical timer or Alexa)
- **Rep counter:** Physical clicker or whiteboard tally
- **Progression:** Add 10 reps/week until 500/500, then add weighted vest

**Rule:** If you miss morning, do it before touching phone. No exceptions.

---

### Deep Work Block #1 (90 Minutes)

**Why:** Device-free mornings prevent algorithmic control of your attention.

**Rules:**
- No phone, no email, no Slack, no dashboard
- Single task only (no multitasking)
- Physical timer set for 90 minutes
- Paper notebook for notes (not digital)

**Weekly Focus Rotation:**

| Day | Focus Area | Example Tasks |
|-----|------------|---------------|
| Monday | **Strategy** | Geographic expansion plan, vertical prioritization, partnership evaluation |
| Tuesday | **Sales** | Script writing, objection handling docs, rep training materials |
| Wednesday | **Product** | Psychocybernet feature design, SaaS MVP scoping, UX improvements |
| Thursday | **Content** | Case studies, documentation, email sequences, LinkedIn posts |
| Friday | **Systems** | Automation builds, bottleneck removal, tool audits |
| Saturday | **Learning** | Book reading, course work, competitive research (structured, not scrolling) |
| Sunday | **Rest** | No deep work. Walk, reflect, family, nature. |

**Outcome:** By Wednesday, you'll have made more strategic progress than most founders do in a month.

### Decision Journal Review (10 Minutes)

**What:** Re-read yesterday's decision entries.

**Questions:**
1. ❓ *"What pattern do I see in my decisions?"*
2. ❓ *"Did fear or greed drive any of them?"*
3. ❓ *"What would I do differently with hindsight?"*

**Why:** Pattern recognition improves with review. You'll spot your weakness triggers.

### Day's Top 3 Decisions Preview (10 Minutes)

**What:** Identify the 3 most important decisions you'll face today.

**Format:**
```
Decision #1: [Brief description]
- Principle at stake: [Which principle?]
- Durov Checklist needed: Yes/No
- Ideal outcome: [What does principle-based look like?]

Decision #2: [Brief description]
- ...

Decision #3: [Brief description]
- ...
```

**Why:** Pre-commitment reduces reactive decision-making.

**Example:**
```
Decision #1: Sales rep asking for 30% commission
- Principle at stake: Fair compensation structure (25% for all)
- Durov Checklist needed: Yes (Principle Check, Long-Term Check)
- Ideal outcome: Hold at 25%, offer performance-based bonuses instead

Decision #2: Whether to buy 100K cheap leads for €500
- Principle at stake: Lead quality over volume
- Durov Checklist needed: Yes (Efficiency Check, Scarcity Check)
- Ideal outcome: Reject, continue enriching 20K quality list

Decision #3: Psychocybernet feature scope (20 ideas, which to build?)
- Principle at stake: Focus over feature bloat
- Durov Checklist needed: Yes (Reframe Check, Scarcity Check)
- Ideal outcome: Build 1 feature delivering 80% value, ship Friday
```

## Component 2: Real-Time Decision Capture

### The Durov Checklist (7 Questions)

**When:** Before any decision with >€1K impact or strategic importance.

**Format:** 7 questions, score each 1-5 (1 = strong no, 5 = strong yes)

| # | Question | Score (1-5) | Notes |
|---|----------|-------------|-------|
| 1 | **Freedom Check:** Am I NOT acting from fear or greed? | ___/5 | |
| 2 | **Principle Check:** Would I walk away to uphold my principle? | ___/5 | |
| 3 | **Long-Term Check:** Will I be proud of this in 5 years? | ___/5 | |
| 4 | **Efficiency Check:** Can this be automated instead of hired? | ___/5 | |
| 5 | **Scarcity Check:** What constraint would make us more creative? | ___/5 | |
| 6 | **Reframe Check:** What's the Option C nobody's considering? | ___/5 | |
| 7 | **Energy Check:** Am I acting first, not waiting to feel ready? | ___/5 | |

**Total Score:** ___/35

**Decision Rule:**
- **30-35:** Green light. Decision is principle-aligned. Execute.
- **25-29:** Yellow light. Proceed with caution. Document reasoning.
- **<25:** Red light. Stop. Sleep on it. Reframe. Consult advisor.

```

### Principle Audit (1 Question)

**The Question:**
> *"Where did I waver from my principles today?"*

**Purpose:** Honest self-assessment without self-judgment.

**Common Answers:**
- *"Checked phone before 8 AM (comfort)"*
- *"Considered buying cheap leads (greed—wanted volume fast)"*
- *"Delayed firing underperforming rep (fear of losing momentum)"*
- *"Skipped deep work block (tiredness, but really discipline failure)"*

**Follow-Up:**
> *"What would Durov do?"*

This forces you to imagine the principle-based response.

### 3. Automation Audit (20 mins)

**Question:** *"What manual work did I or my team do this week that could be automated?"*

**Template:**
```
Task | Current Time/Wk | Automation Possible? | Build Time | ROI
-----|-----------------|----------------------|------------|-----

Reply monitoring | 3 hrs | Yes (Hermes webhook) | 3 hrs | 1 week
CRM updates | 5 hrs | Yes (Browser Use) | 8 hrs | 2 weeks
```

**Rule:** If ROI < 4 weeks, build it this week.

---

### 4. B-Player Identification (20 mins)

**Question:** *"Who on my team (or in my life) is a B-player?"*

**Definition:** B-players are those who:
- Can't focus on one task for extended periods
- Require management/oversight
- Demotivate A-players around them
- Invent non-existent problems to look busy

**Template:**
```
B-Player: [Name/Role]

Evidence:
- [Specific behavior 1]
- [Specific behavior 2]

Impact:
- [How they're dragging down the team]

Action:
- [ ] 2-week improvement plan (specific metrics)
- [ ] Replace immediately (pipeline ready)
- [ ] Acceptable (core values aligned, just developing)

Timeline: [Date for decision]
```

**Rule:** If you're hesitating, that's fear. Set the timeline. Execute.

---

**Question:** *"What artificial scarcity would make us more creative next week?"*

**Template:**
```

## Common Failure Modes (And How to Avoid Them)

### Failure Mode 1: Skipping Morning Routine
**Why:** Tired, "just one more hour of sleep"

**Antidote:** Phone in another room. Alarm clock (physical). No negotiation.

**Durov Quote:** *"The main muscle you exercise is self-discipline."*

---

### Failure Mode 2: Not Logging Decisions
**Why:** "Too busy," "This one doesn't matter"

**Antidote:** If decision is >€1K impact or strategic, it matters. Log it in <2 mins via Telegram.

**Rule:** Better logged imperfectly than not logged at all.

---

### Failure Mode 3: Dishonest Self-Scoring
**Why:** Ego doesn't want to admit fear/greed

**Antidote:** Nobody sees your scores but you. Lie to yourself = break the system.

**Question:** *"Would I show this journal to Durov?"*

---

### Failure Mode 4: Skipping Weekly Review
**Why:** "Too much work," "I'll do it tomorrow"

**Antidote:** Sunday is non-negotiable. Schedule it like a board meeting.

**Reframe:** 2 hours on Sunday saves 10 hours of wasted effort during the week.

---

### Failure Mode 5: Perfectionism
**Why:** Missed one day, "I've failed, might as well quit"

**Antidote:** One missed day ≠ failure. Resume immediately. Compound effect still works.

**Math:** 90% consistency = 324/365 days = elite level. Aim for 90%, not 100%.
