# The Durov Hierarchy — Quick Reference

## 🏆 The One Principle
> **"Freedom matters more than money."** (Свобода дороже денег)

**Test:** Would I rather lose this deal/person/revenue stream than compromise my freedom?
- If **Yes** → It's a real principle. Uphold it.
- If **No** → It's a preference. Reconsider.

---

## 🎯 Two Enemies of Freedom

| Enemy | Symptom | Antidote |
|-------|---------|----------|
| **Fear** | Reacting defensively, choosing safety over vision | Ask: *"What would I do if I couldn't fail?"* |
| **Greed** | Cutting corners, chasing quick money over long-term value | Ask: *"Will I be proud of this in 5 years?"* |

**Decision Rule:** If a choice is driven by fear OR greed → **STOP**. Reframe from first principles.

---

## 💪 Three Daily Disciplines (Non-Negotiable)

1. **300 push-ups + 300 squats** (before checking phone)
2. **Device-free mornings** (first 90 minutes = deep work)
3. **18-hour fasting** (eat in 6-hour window, no processed sugar)

**Why:** *"The main muscle you exercise is self-discipline. Once it's strong, everything else follows."*

---

## 🧠 Four Decision Filters (Run Before Any Major Choice)

1. **Freedom Check:** Am I NOT acting from fear or greed?
2. **Principle Check:** Would I walk away to uphold this?
3. **Efficiency Check:** Can this be automated instead of hired?
4. **Long-Term Check:** Will I be proud of this in 5 years?

**Score:** 4/4 = Execute. <4 = Stop. Reframe. Sleep on it.

## 🏗️ Five Business Principles

1. **Small Team, Massive Automation** (<10 core team until €500K/mo)
2. **Principles > Profit** (willing to shut down vs. compromise)
3. **Scarcity Breeds Creativity** (artificial constraints = innovation)
4. **Fire B-Players Fast** (they demotivate A-players)
5. **Option C Always Exists** (reframe false dilemmas)

---

## ⚡ Six Productivity Rules

1. **No phone first 90 minutes** (define what's important, don't let algorithms define it)
2. **Single-task deep work** (90-min blocks, no interruptions)
3. **Action → Energy** (don't wait to feel ready; act first, motivation follows)
4. **Say no to good opportunities** (focus on great ones only)
5. **Weekly principle audit** (where did I waver this week?)
6. **Live on bonus time** (you could die tomorrow; build something that matters)

## 🧭 Nine Questions for Weekly Review (Sunday, 2 hours)

1. What revenue did I generate this week?
2. What principles did I uphold? Where did I waver?
3. What was my best decision? Worst?
4. What can be automated before hiring anyone?
5. Who is a B-player I need to fire/replace?
6. What constraint would make us more creative next week?
7. What false dilemma did I accept? What's Option C?
8. Did I act first, or wait to feel ready?
9. Am I living on bonus time, or operating from scarcity mindset?
